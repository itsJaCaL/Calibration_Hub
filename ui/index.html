JaCaL's Calibration Wizard

The 21-Step Path to Perfection

We will calibrate the hardware, dial in the firmware, and finally tune the melted plastic.

🔴 THE ABSOLUTE NECESSITIES

  • OrcaSlicer (Recommended): Use the "Calibration" menu at the top. It builds the tests and G-code for you so you don't have to think.
  • 1x Roll of Filament: You can't tune plastic without plastic!
  • Digital Calipers: Required to tune Flow Rate and Skew down to 0.01mm.
  • Steel Ruler: At least 150mm. Required for Kinematics.
  • A4 Paper: For physical Z-Offset.
  • Marker / Sharpie: To mark filament for E-Steps.

🟡 HIGHLY RECOMMENDED

  • Belt Frequency App: (e.g., Spectroid)
  • ADXL345 Accelerometer: For Input Shaper.

Step 1: Sensor Check

1. The Clicky Switches (Endstops)

We need to prove your printer knows when it hits a wall.

  1. Without touching the printer, click Query Endstops below. They should all say open.
  2. Use your left hand to press and HOLD DOWN the X-axis physical switch on your printer.
  3. While holding it down, use your mouse to click Query Endstops again.
  4. The screen should now say the X axis is TRIGGERED.
  5. Let go of the switch. Repeat this exact process for the Y and Z switches.

Fix it: If a switch says TRIGGERED when you aren't touching it, you need to open printer.cfg and put an exclamation mark ( ! ) in front of its pin name.

Press Query to check endstops...

2. Probe / BLTouch (The Finger Test)

  1. Click 👇 Down to drop the pin.
  2. Click ❓ Query. The status below should say open.
  3. Gently push the pin up with your finger until it clicks.
  4. Click ❓ Query. It should now say TRIGGERED.
  5. The probe is now latched in an error state (likely flashing red). Click ♻️ Reset to clear its brain.
  6. Click 👇 Down to drop the pin.
  7. Click 👆 Up to pull the pin safely back up.

⚠️ Repeat this entire sequence one more time to make absolutely sure it works reliably!

Probe status unknown...

Step 2: Motion Check

1. The Wiggle Test (Power Check)

⚠️ CRITICAL: Use your hands to physically push the print head to the exact center of the bed right now! If you don't, it might crash into a wall during this test.

We need to prove the motors have power and aren't wired backwards.

  1. Click the Buzz X button below.
  2. Watch the X motor. It should smoothly wiggle back and forth in place.
  3. It should NOT make a horrible screeching or grinding noise.
  4. Repeat this for the Y, Z, and Extruder motors.
Loading motors...

2. Travel Direction

⚠️ CRITICAL: Use your hands to physically push the print head to the exact center of the bed right now!

  1. Click Move X Right. The print head MUST move toward the Right side.
  2. Click Move Y Positive. If your printer moves the bed (Bed-Slinger), the bed should move Forward (towards you). If your printer moves the head instead, the head should move Back (away from you).
  3. Note: If your head moves diagonally, don't panic! It just means your printer uses CoreXY logic, but it should still generally head towards the Right/Back.

Step 3: Kinematics (The Ruler Test)

⚠️ Warning: If you clicked move too many times and hit a wall, click Unlock Motors below, move the head back to the center by hand, and start over.

1. The Measurement Check

We need to prove that when you ask for 50mm, the printer physically moves exactly 50mm.

  1. Lay your Steel Ruler flat on the print bed along the X-axis (left to right).
  2. Align the nozzle so it is pointing directly at the 0mm mark.
  3. Click Move X +50mm below.
  4. Look at the ruler. The nozzle should now be pointing exactly at the 50mm mark.
  5. Repeat this for the Y-axis (front to back) and the Z-axis (up and down).

Fix it: If it moves 100mm or only 25mm, your rotation_distance in printer.cfg is wrong. You likely have the wrong belt pitch or motor steps selected.



Step 4: Belt Tension

1. The Guitar String Method

If your belts are loose, your circles will look like eggs. If they are too tight, your motors will scream and die.

  1. Physically move your toolhead so there is a long, clear section of belt exposed.
  2. Open a Frequency App (like Spectroid) on your phone.
  3. Pluck the belt with your finger like a guitar string.
  4. The app will show a "peak" frequency (Hz). Aim for ~110Hz to 150Hz.
  5. Pluck the other belt. Tighten or loosen the screws until both belts hum at the same frequency.

Tip: It doesn't matter if you choose 110Hz or 140Hz, what matters is that X and Y are IDENTICAL.



Step 5: Thermal Tuning (PID)

1. The Science of Heat

We need to teach your printer how to hold a temperature without wobbling up and down.

  1. Click Tune Bed. The printer will heat up and cool down 5 times. This can take up to 30 minutes
  2. Wait for the graph to flatten out completely. The temperature line MUST be stable before you continue.
  3. Repeat this for the Hotend.
  4. CRITICAL: Once both are done, click 💾 Save Config. The printer will restart. Do not close this page
Ready.

Step 6: Extruder (The 100mm Test)

1. The Measurement

  1. Click 🔥 Heat to 210°C and wait for the nozzle to get hot.
  2. Use your Steel Ruler to measure exactly 120mm of filament from where it enters the extruder.
  3. Use your Marker to put a clear line on the filament at that 120mm mark.
  4. Click ⚙️ Extrude 100mm. The printer will pull the filament slowly.
  5. Once it stops, measure the distance from the extruder to your mark.
  6. Expected: 20mm. Math: 120 - (What's left) = Actual Extruded.

Step 7: Motor Power (Currents)

1. The Safety Limit

If you push too much power into the motors, they will get hot enough to melt your printer. We need to set a safety limit.

  1. Find the sticker on your motor. Look for a number ending in A (Amps), like 1.5A.
  2. Type that number into the box below.
  3. Click Calculate. It will give you a safe run_current.
  4. Open your printer.cfg and update the run_current for that motor. Repeat for all motors


Step 8: Bed Alignment

1. The Leveling Check

We need to make sure the bed is actually flat relative to the nozzle.

  1. Click 🏠 Home All. The printer will move to its start position.
  2. If you have 2 or more Z motors, click Auto-Align.
  3. If you have manual knobs under the bed, click Check Bed Screws and follow the instructions in the console.

Step 9: Z-Offset (The Paper Test)

1. The First Layer Squish

We need to set the exact distance between the nozzle and the bed so your plastic sticks.

  1. Place a single sheet of A4 Paper on the bed under the nozzle.
  2. Click Start Paper Test. The nozzle will move to the center and drop down.
  3. Wiggle the paper back and forth. If it moves freely, click Down -0.1.
  4. Keep clicking Down until the nozzle grabs the paper.
  5. The Goal: You should feel the nozzle "biting" the paper. You can move it, but there is friction.
  6. Click Accept, then click Save Config.
Ready for Paper Test...

Step 10: Axis Twist (The Straightness Test)

1. The Bent Rail Fix

If your X-axis rail is slightly twisted, your bed will never feel level. We are going to "digitally" straighten it.

  1. Click Start Calibration. The nozzle will move to 3 or more points on the bed.
  2. At EVERY POINT, repeat the Paper Test (Step 9).
  3. Wiggle the paper, adjust height until it "bites," and click Accept Point.
  4. Once all points are done, click Save Config.
Ready for Twist Check...

Step 11: Bed Mesh (The Digital Map)

1. The Topography Map

No print bed is perfectly flat. We are going to map the hills and valleys so the printer can adjust live while printing.

  1. Click Generate Map.
  2. The printer will probe the bed in a grid (e.g., 5x5 points).
  3. Do not touch the printer while it is probing.
  4. Once finished, click Save Config.
Ready to probe...

Step 15: Temp Tower (The Heat Test)

1. How to generate the tower

You cannot just download a tower model and print it normally. The printer needs specific G-code to change the heat at every level.

  • OrcaSlicer: Top Menu ➔ Calibration ➔ Temperature. (It generates the model and G-code for you).
  • SuperSlicer: Top Menu ➔ Calibration ➔ Temperature Calibration.
  • Cura: Click Marketplace (top right) ➔ Install "AutoTowers Generator". Restart Cura, then go to Extensions ➔ AutoTowers.

2. Which temp is best?

Every brand of filament is different. Once it finishes printing, look for:

  1. The section with the least stringing.
  2. The section with the shiniest, cleanest finish.
  3. The section where the bridges (horizontal gaps) don't sag.
  4. Write the winning temperature down and save it in your Slicer's filament profile.

  • Top Layers: 0

Measure the wall thickness at the VERY TOP of the cube. Avoid the bottom "bulge"!

Step 17: Pressure Advance (Sharp Corners)

1. Generating the Test

Pressure Advance (PA) stops corners from bulging. We need to print a PA Tower or Line test.

  • OrcaSlicer (Easiest): Top Menu ➔ Calibration ➔ Pressure Advance. Choose "PA Tower" or "Line". It does all the math for you. Skip the Klipper command below.
  • Other Slicers: Download a PA Tower STL. Before you hit print, paste this into your Mainsail Console:
TUNING_TOWER COMMAND=SET_PRESSURE_ADVANCE PARAMETER=ADVANCE START=0 FACTOR=.005

(Change FACTOR to .020 if you use a Bowden tube).

Ready for height...

Step 18: Max Volumetric Flow (The Speed Limit)

1. How fast can you melt?

If you try to print faster than the hotend can melt plastic, the extruder will "click" and strip the filament.

  • OrcaSlicer: Top Menu ➔ Calibration ➔ Max Flowrate.
  • Other Slicers: Search CNC Kitchen Max Flow Test online.
  1. Print the test. It will print a block and pump more plastic every few layers.
  2. Watch the surface. When it starts looking dull, rough, or has holes, that is your limit.
  3. Measure the height where it failed, do the math (or let OrcaSlicer tell you), and save that "Max Volumetric Speed" in your filament profile.

Step 19: Retraction Distance

1. Pulling it back

Retraction pulls the filament back so it doesn't leak while the head is moving. We need to find the right distance.

  • OrcaSlicer: Top Menu ➔ Calibration ➔ Retraction Test.
  • Cura: Extensions ➔ AutoTowers ➔ Retract Tower.
  1. Print the tower.
  2. Look for the lowest height section with ZERO "hairs" (strings).
  3. Note the Distance (e.g., 0.8mm for Direct Drive, 5mm for Bowden) and save it in your Slicer.

Step 20: Retraction Speed

Retracting too slow leaves strings. Retracting too fast causes air bubbles and clogs. Find the "sweet spot" (usually 30mm/s to 60mm/s).

  1. Generate another Retraction Tower, but this time, set the slicer to change the Speed instead of the distance. (In Orca, it's the second Retraction calibration option).
  2. Choose the cleanest result. Update your Slicer settings.

Step 21: The Final Trophy (Tolerance)

1. Is it perfect?

This is the "Final Boss." We print a set of pegs with different gaps (0.1mm to 0.5mm) to see how accurate your printer is.

  1. Download and print the Tolerance Test below.
  2. Wait for it to cool, then try to pull or twist the pegs out.
  3. If you can free the 0.1mm or 0.2mm peg, your printer is Legendary.
  4. If you can only free the 0.5mm peg, go back to Step 16 and check your Flow Rate again. You are likely over-extruding.
